# Quatro Intelligence Services — Naming System

**Date:** April 1, 2026
**Purpose:** Define the Quatro-native naming system for all intelligence services the network provides. These names replace any DataOS product catalog names, any tier-sequential labels, and any inconsistent naming across the current site.
**Status:** Draft for alignment

---

## Naming Principles

1. **Two words maximum.** The buyer should be able to say "I want Grid Health" in a sentence without stumbling. If a name needs three words to be clear, it's too complex.

2. **No suffix words.** Drop "Intelligence," "Product," "Platform," "Module," "Solution," "System" from every name. The buyer already knows they're getting intelligence — that's what the network provides. Adding "Intelligence" to every name is like a restaurant putting "Food" after every menu item.

3. **Outcome-named, not mechanism-named.** "Revenue Assurance" (outcome the buyer wants) not "Financial Reconciliation Engine" (mechanism that delivers it). "Grid Health" (what the buyer cares about) not "SCADA Analytics Module" (what the technology does).

4. **Service framing, not product framing.** These are services the network provides — like a telecom network provides Voice, Data, Messaging. The buyer subscribes to Grid Health the way they subscribe to broadband. They don't "buy the Grid Health product" — they "connect Grid Health."

5. **Industry-recognizable.** Names should use terms the buyer's industry already uses. "Revenue Assurance" is a recognized category in telecom/satellite. "Space Domain Awareness" is a recognized term in defense. Don't invent new language when the industry already has the right words.

6. **No traceability to DataOS.** Names must not appear in the DataOS product catalog, positioning documents, or outbound materials. If a name exists in both Quatro and DataOS contexts, it needs to be different enough that a buyer Googling the term doesn't find DataOS marketing.

7. **No tier labels.** No service name includes or implies See / Understand / Orchestrate. Every service delivers the full sensing-to-action loop for its domain.

---

## The Naming Pattern

```
[Domain Noun] + [Outcome Noun]
```

Examples: Grid Health. Revenue Assurance. Pipeline Integrity. Fleet Health.

Some services are specific enough that the domain noun IS the name: Water Systems. Airport Operations. Port Operations. These work because the domain is self-evident and the service scope is clear.

## Naming Decisions Log

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Drop "Intelligence" suffix everywhere | Redundant. The network provides intelligence — adding it to every name is noise. |
| "Asset Monitoring" → "Asset Health" | "Monitoring" implies passive watching (See tier). "Health" implies prediction and proactive management (full loop). |
| "Satellite Revenue Assurance" → "Revenue Assurance" | Removes satellite-specific framing. Revenue leakage from operational events exists in every industry. |
| "Hybrid Service Assurance" → "Service Assurance" | "Hybrid" is architecture language. The buyer doesn't care whether the transport is hybrid — they care about end-to-end SLA visibility. |
| "Grid Operations" → "Grid Health" | Same logic as Asset Health. "Operations" describes what the buyer does. "Health" describes what the service delivers. |
| "Operational Intelligence" (defense) → "Mission Intelligence" | "Operational Intelligence" is too generic — it's Quatro's brand descriptor, not a service name. "Mission" signals the defense buyer and is specific enough to be meaningful. |
| "Pipeline Security" → "Pipeline Integrity" | "Integrity" is the recognized industry term (pipeline integrity management). "Security" implies only physical security. Integrity covers structural health, leak detection, corrosion, AND security. |
| Keep "Space Domain Awareness" for Networks | Recognized defense/space term. Changing it loses industry credibility. |
| Add "Domain Awareness" for National Security | Generalizes space domain awareness to land/maritime/air/cyber for the defense buyer who operates across all domains. |
| Add "Storm Response" as a standalone service | Currently a capability buried in the energy page. It's significant enough (automated crew pre-positioning, mutual aid) to be a named service. |
| Add "Operator Guidance" as cross-industry | Currently a capability on industry pages. It's the fusion-in-action service — operational data + semantic context = contextual recommendations. Deserves its own name. |
| Add "Facility Operations" | Currently a use case (Smart Facilities). Elevating it to a named service because building intelligence is a growing market segment (smart cities, campuses, hospitals). |

## How Services Appear on the Site

### The Network Page (Section 4)

Services are grouped by industry domain. The buyer scans for their industry, sees 3-6 services, and clicks through to the solution page for detail. No tier labels. No "See" / "Understand" badges.

```
Energy:          Grid Health · Pipeline Integrity · Storm Response · Energy Compliance
Networks:        Network Operations · Service Assurance · Fleet Health · Revenue Assurance · Teleport Operations · Space Domain Awareness
National Security: Mission Intelligence · Physical Security · Domain Awareness
Infrastructure:  Water Systems · Environmental Compliance · Airport Operations · Port Operations · Corridor Intelligence · Asset Health · Facility Operations
Finance:         Credit Risk · Claims Classification · NPA Early Warning · Customer 360 · Regulatory Compliance
```

Cross-industry services (Asset Health, Fleet Logistics, Revenue Assurance, Operator Guidance) appear in a separate "Available to any industry" row or as badges on the services that span verticals.

### Industry Pages

Each industry page lists its services in the capabilities section — but without tier labels. The `tier: see/understand/orchestrate` field in the content YAML gets replaced with a simple `depth: core/extended` or removed entirely. Capabilities are grouped by what the service does, not by which tier they belong to.

### Solution Pages (Individual Service Pages)

Each service gets its own page (already exists for most). The `platformMapping` field (which currently references See/Understand/Orchestrate) gets replaced with a narrative description of what the service delivers — or removed in favor of a capabilities list that doesn't reference tiers.

## Traceability Check Against DataOS Product Catalog

The DataOS Positioning Update (March 2026) lists these named products:
- Fleet Health Intelligence → Quatro uses "Fleet Health" (no suffix — different enough)
- Ground Station 360 → Not used in Quatro naming (absorbed into Teleport Operations)
- Space Domain Awareness → Same name — BUT this is a recognized defense term, not a DataOS invention. Acceptable.
- Revenue Assurance → Same name — recognized industry category (telecom/satellite). Acceptable.
- MissionOS → Quatro uses "Mission Intelligence" (different name)
- Cybersecurity Compliance Intelligence → Not used in Quatro naming
- Supply Chain Intelligence for Space → Not used in Quatro naming
- M&A Integration Intelligence → Not used in Quatro naming

**Risk assessment:** Revenue Assurance and Space Domain Awareness appear in both catalogs. Both are recognized industry terms that predate DataOS — a buyer searching these terms will find industry content, not DataOS marketing specifically. The risk of traceability is low. If concerned, "Revenue Assurance" could become "Revenue Protection" and "Space Domain Awareness" could become "Orbital Awareness" — but this sacrifices industry-recognized terminology for minimal traceability gain.

**Recommendation:** Keep both names. They belong to the industry, not to DataOS.
