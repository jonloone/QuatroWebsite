## The Invisible Extraction

Illegal mining operations strip natural resources and devastate ecosystems across remote territories. Clandestine pipeline tapping extracts crude oil from national infrastructure. Deforestation accelerates in protected areas. The environmental damage is severe — but the fiscal damage is equally devastating. Every barrel stolen, every hectare cleared, every mineral extracted without authorization represents direct revenue loss to the national treasury.

These operations are not isolated crimes. They fund the same criminal networks that traffic drugs, launder money, and recruit youth. Illegal mining has become a primary revenue source for transnational criminal organizations — financing operations that extend far beyond the extraction site itself. The revenue from a single illegal mining operation can sustain an entire urban criminal network for months.

Traditional enforcement is reactive. A patrol discovers a site that has been operating for months. By then, the ore has been extracted, the trees have been felled, the pipeline has been tapped hundreds of times. The operators have moved. The environmental damage is irreversible. The evidence is degraded. The fiscal loss is permanent.

What's needed is persistent surveillance — the ability to detect new activity within hours of its start, generate enforcement-grade intelligence automatically, and target the network that operates the site — not just the site itself.

## Detection Capabilities

Six integrated detection capabilities — from space to ground to sea — that provide continuous surveillance across every resource domain.

### 01 — Satellite Change Detection

Daily satellite earth observation with AI-powered change detection. Optical and synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery with daily revisit cadence. AI models trained specifically on mining, deforestation, and construction signatures identify new activity within hours of occurrence.

Multi-spectral analysis detects environmental contamination — soil disturbance patterns, water quality changes, vegetation stress — that precedes or accompanies illegal extraction. Change detection algorithms compare current imagery against historical baselines, flagging anomalies that exceed defined thresholds automatically.

The output is not raw imagery for analysts to review manually. It is prioritized detection events — each tagged with location, classification, confidence score, and recommended response — delivered to enforcement coordinators on the same day the activity is detected.

**Partner:** NOVI Space — satellite earth observation, environmental monitoring, IUU fishing detection.

### 02 — Pipeline Integrity Intelligence

Vibration detection, pressure analysis, and E-RTTM (Extended Real-Time Transient Modeling) hydraulic analysis that identifies clandestine tapping and theft events in real time. Every unauthorized intervention on a pipeline produces a hydraulic signature — a pressure transient, a flow anomaly, a vibration pattern — that the detection system identifies and classifies.

Evidence-grade detection means every event is logged with timestamp, location estimate, magnitude, and hydraulic signature. The detection record constitutes evidence for regulatory enforcement and criminal prosecution — meeting national regulatory standards and law enforcement chain-of-custody requirements.

Fiber optic intrusion detection along pipeline corridors provides perimeter awareness — detecting physical access to pipeline infrastructure before tapping occurs. Combined with hydraulic analysis, the system identifies both the attempt and the extraction.

**Partner:** ECR Platform X — E-RTTM hydraulic leak and theft detection for pipeline infrastructure.

### 03 — Environmental Monitoring

Real-time sensor networks for water quality, air quality, and soil contamination. IoT environmental sensors deployed at extraction sites, downstream water courses, and protected area boundaries. Automated compliance monitoring against national environmental and resource regulatory standards.

Contamination events are correlated with extraction activity — when a mining operation starts, the environmental impact appears in downstream water quality data within hours to days. The correlation connects environmental damage to specific extraction sites and specific operators.

Environmental intelligence is not separate from enforcement intelligence. It is the evidence layer that quantifies the damage — providing the regulatory basis for intervention and the prosecution basis for penalties.

### 04 — Dark Vessel Detection

AIS/VMS vessel tracking with satellite correlation identifies vessels operating without transponders. Transponder gap analysis detects when vessels disable identification systems upon entering restricted waters — the signature of illegal fishing, unauthorized extraction, or maritime smuggling.

Route prediction algorithms forecast where dark vessels are heading based on historical patterns, current conditions, and network intelligence. Enforcement targeting packages include vessel identification, predicted route, estimated arrival, and correlated intelligence from other domains.

Real-world applications include protection of marine reserves from IUU (Illegal, Unreported, Unregulated) fishing and enforcement of exclusive economic zones. The same detection capability applies to continental shelf mineral extraction, offshore pipeline approaches, and maritime smuggling routes.

### 05 — Deforestation Tracking

Satellite-based canopy loss detection across protected areas and buffer zones. Multi-temporal analysis compares vegetation indices over time — identifying not just where clearing has occurred, but the rate and pattern of clearing that indicates organized, commercial-scale deforestation versus subsistence activity.

Automated alerts trigger when clearing exceeds permitted thresholds — whether defined by concession boundaries, protected area regulations, or environmental compliance agreements. Evidence packages document the progression of clearing with dated imagery, area calculations, and regulatory context.

Correlation with road construction detection identifies the infrastructure that precedes large-scale deforestation — new access roads into previously inaccessible areas are the earliest indicator of planned extraction.

### 06 — Enforcement Intelligence

Detection events correlated with criminal network intelligence. Satellite detections, pipeline alerts, environmental anomalies, and maritime events are not treated as isolated incidents. They are correlated with network intelligence to answer the enforcement questions: Who operates this site? How are resources transported? Where do proceeds enter the financial system? Who provides protection?

Enforcement intelligence maps the full operational chain — from extraction to transport to sale to money laundering — connecting the physical activity detected by sensors to the organizational structure identified by network analysis. The output is not just a detection alert. It is an enforcement package: who, where, how, and the evidence chain to prosecute.

## Multi-Source Intelligence Fusion

Three sensor layers — space, ground, and maritime — fused through one intelligence engine into a single operational picture.

### Space Layer

Daily revisit optical and SAR satellite imagery. AI change detection trained on mining, deforestation, and construction signatures. Multi-spectral analysis for environmental contamination — soil disturbance, water quality, vegetation stress. NOVI Space provides the satellite earth observation backbone.

### Ground Layer

Pipeline SCADA systems. Vibration and pressure sensors. Flow rate monitors. Environmental IoT sensors for water and air quality. Fiber optic intrusion detection along pipeline corridors. ECR Platform X provides DevSafeOps capability — E-RTTM hydraulic detection, predictive analytics, and operational decisioning support for pipeline integrity. DataMiner manages the high-volume time-series data ingestion and correlation natively.

### Maritime Layer

AIS/VMS vessel tracking. Radar integration. Dark vessel identification through transponder gap analysis. Satellite correlation for vessels operating without identification. Route prediction and enforcement targeting for IUU fishing and maritime resource theft.

### Intelligence Layer

Detection events from all three sensor layers are correlated automatically. A satellite detection of new mining activity triggers a query of financial intelligence for the area. A pipeline theft event is correlated with dark vessel activity at the nearest port. An environmental contamination alert is linked to upstream extraction operations.

The intelligence layer is where detection becomes enforcement. Raw sensor data becomes prioritized, classified, attributed detection events — each connected to the network, financial, and operational context that turns a technical alert into an actionable enforcement package.
