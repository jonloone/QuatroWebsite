## Overview

In cities where youth homicide rates claim 25-30% of all victims, criminal organizations deliberately target children as young as 12 for recruitment. Quatro powers the Youth Recruitment Intelligence Framework -- a 36-month phased deployment that unifies school records, health data, security intelligence, and community program enrollment into a predictive early warning system. The platform computes a Youth Vulnerability Index across seven risk dimensions, generates automated alerts, and coordinates multi-agency interventions so that vulnerable youth are connected to life-saving programs within days of identification.

## Who This Is For

- **Municipal Safety Directors** accountable for homicide reduction who need measurable evidence of which prevention strategies work, shifting budget discussions from reactive policing to proven intervention programs.
- **School Principals** who can identify struggling students but lack the coordination infrastructure to connect them to community services before recruitment occurs.
- **Community Organization Directors** with limited staff who need targeted referrals of highest-need youth rather than serving only those who self-identify.
