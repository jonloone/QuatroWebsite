## The Challenge

Youth violence in Latin America's coastal cities claims hundreds of young lives annually. Yet the systems designed to prevent it remain fragmented: schools report incidents independently, health clinics see trauma without context, social workers lack real-time visibility into risk patterns, and justice records sit in isolation. By the time any single institution recognizes a crisis, it's too late.

## The Operational Intelligence Solution

Quatro integrates school records, health data, community violence reports, and justice system feeds into a unified platform. The Youth Vulnerability Index — an AI-powered composite risk score — identifies at-risk youth before violence erupts. Early-warning alerts coordinate social workers, mentors, and community organizations around unified intervention strategies.

**Key outcomes:**
- 1,200+ at-risk youth identified and enrolled in coordinated programs within Year 1
- 50+ early-warning alerts per month triggering proactive intervention
- 8–12 week average enrollment window from identification to program entry
- Governance dashboards demonstrating impact and donor accountability

## Implementation Path

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):** Data integration and Youth Vulnerability Index calibration
**Phase 2 (Weeks 5–12):** Alert protocols and case management system deployment
**Phase 3 (Weeks 13–52):** Full coordination across schools, health, community, and justice
**Phases 4–36:** Scaling to additional cities and outcome measurement

The 4-week proof of value is credited toward the full contract, de-risking the engagement.

## Why Cities Choose Quatro

Operational intelligence transforms scattered institutional data into coordinated action. You don't ask social workers to be better detectives; you give them earlier, better information so interventions can happen before tragedy does. Privacy and governance are built in, not added later.
