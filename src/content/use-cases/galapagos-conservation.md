## Overview

The Galapagos Islands represent a UNESCO World Heritage site of planetary significance, yet operational monitoring has been constrained by a vast coverage area and minimal staffing. Quatro delivers the Conservation Intelligence platform -- a unified maritime and terrestrial monitoring system that cross-references real-time vessel data against registration databases, detects unauthorized terrestrial activity through cameras and satellite imagery, and correlates observations across sources into actionable incident reports. A 90-day pilot on Santa Cruz island establishes proof of concept for expansion to the full archipelago and integration with Ecuador's Pacific EEZ maritime security apparatus.

## Who This Is For

- **GNP Park Directors** responsible for protecting irreplaceable ecosystems with limited resources, who need force multiplication technology to achieve complete visibility without expanding staff.
- **Ecuador's Ministry of Environment** requiring operational intelligence to enforce Galapagos protections, coordinate with the Navy and coast guard, and present evidence at UNESCO reviews.
- **International Conservation Partners** funding conservation work who need real-time threat data and a replicable model for advanced monitoring at other UNESCO World Heritage marine sites.
