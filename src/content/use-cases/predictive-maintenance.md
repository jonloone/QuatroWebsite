## Overview

Upstream E&P operations in remote and harsh environments are unforgiving. Electric Submersible Pumps, rod pumps, compressors, and wellheads operate under extreme pressure and temperature variations, sometimes hundreds of miles from the nearest service location. When equipment fails, the cost is not just the repair -- it is the mobilization time, lost production revenue, and the risk of cascading damage to adjacent equipment.

Quatro transforms maintenance from reactive to predictive by monitoring every production asset continuously through the SMARTs Edge Gateway. The gateway sits at harsh remote locations, pre-filtering anomalies and compressing data before transmission over satellite or radio links, ensuring real-time monitoring even where broadband is impossible. The Intelligence Platform then applies failure prediction models with 7-14 day advance warnings, condition-based scheduling, and crew route optimization to ensure interventions happen before catastrophic breakdown.

The result is a shift from time-based maintenance cycles that over-service healthy equipment and miss stressed assets to condition-based scheduling that sends crews only where and when they are actually needed.

## Key Outcomes

- **ESP downtime per incident** reduced from 8-12 days to 2-3 days
- **Unplanned failures** cut from 8-12 to 2-3 incidents per year
- **Maintenance cost per asset** reduced by 30% through condition-based scheduling
- **Equipment lifespan** extended by 20% with planned maintenance replacing reactive repairs
- **Truck roll efficiency** improved from 1-2 wells per visit to 4-6 wells per visit
- **Mean time to repair** reduced from 8-12 hours to 3-4 hours via remote diagnostics
- **Maintenance prediction accuracy** improved from 60-70% to 92%+
