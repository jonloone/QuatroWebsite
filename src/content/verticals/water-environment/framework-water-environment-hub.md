## Section 2 — INDUSTRY CONTEXT (2-3 sentences, "why now")

> **Framework rule:** Name the industry moment. Establish credibility: "this page was built by people who know our world."

**Label:** `THE INFRASTRUCTURE INTELLIGENCE CRISIS`

**Content (PAS — Problem, stated as industry moment):**

Water systems in Latin America face convergence of acute challenges: aging distribution infrastructure that loses 40-50% of treated water to undetected leaks; regulatory mandates from SENAGUA requiring real-time quality compliance across hundreds of monitoring points; climate adaptation pressures requiring predictive system health and resilience planning; and ESG reporting demands creating new compliance burdens on utilities and industrial operators.

This isn't unique to Ecuador. Across every water-dependent region — from municipal systems managing thousands of kilometers of pipe to agricultural operators protecting water quality in watershed areas — the same pattern repeats. Fragmented sensor data. Regulatory systems disconnected from operational systems. Manual compliance reporting. No visibility into environmental impacts until damage is already done.

*Framework note: This is the PAS "Problem" beat — names the pain the buyer already feels using concrete, quantified context (40-50% water loss) and regulatory specificity (SENAGUA, ESG). The specificity of the regulatory environment establishes domain credibility.*

## Section 3 — PROBLEM SECTION (PAS — Agitation)

> **Framework rule:** Three-act temporal story with escalating consequence. This section should be PROSE, not cards or columns. Stories create urgency. Cards create comprehension. At the top of a landing page, urgency wins.

**Content (PAS — Agitation, escalating consequence):**

A leak starts in a distribution line serving 50,000 residents. The system operator doesn't know it exists until three days later when water pressure drops and customers complain. By then, millions of liters of treated water — and revenue — are lost. The operator has no idea where the leak is. No automated pressure models. No correlation between system state changes and network topology. Finding the leak takes a field crew a week. The leak persists for a month before repair. Total water loss: unrecoverable. Total revenue loss: quantified in quarterly audits.

Environmental operators face the same asymmetry. A mining operation upstream starts leaching heavy metals into a protected watershed. The environmental authority doesn't detect water quality degradation until it appears in fish kills downstream — weeks later. By then, evidence is degraded, damage is irreversible, and prosecution requires months of manual data assembly.

Quatro eliminates the gap. One utility connects every water meter, every pressure sensor, every water quality monitor, and every environmental data source into a single operational picture — with real-time anomaly detection, predictive leak localization, automated compliance reporting, and full audit trails for enforcement. Operators see problems before they cascade. Regulators see violations before they cause damage.

*Framework note: The leak story quantifies the cost (water loss, revenue loss, detection time). The environmental story establishes cross-stakeholder value. Prose format per framework instruction. The solution positioning (Quatro eliminates the gap) follows as the resolution.*
