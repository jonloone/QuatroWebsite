## Section 2 — INDUSTRY CONTEXT (2-3 sentences, "why now")

> **Framework rule:** Name the industry moment. Establish credibility: "this page was built by people who know our world."

**Label:** `THE CONVERGENCE MOMENT`

**Content (PAS — Problem, stated as industry moment):**

The satellite industry is experiencing the same convergence that reshaped telecommunications, energy, and defense: fragmented legacy systems designed for a slower, simpler era are colliding with the real-time demands of modern operations. Ground segments were built around separate Monitoring & Control (M&C) tools and separate Operations Support Systems (OSS/BSS) — each vendor's equipment managed independently, each system holding a piece of the operational picture. When a transponder anomaly occurs, the RF monitoring tool sees it. The billing system doesn't. When a constellation-wide maintenance window begins, the provisioning system knows, but the anomaly detection system doesn't adjust its thresholds. The problem compounds with scale: add LEO/MEO alongside GEO, add commercial and defense missions in the same infrastructure, add pressure to reduce operational cost while increasing service reliability — and the fragmented approach breaks down entirely.

This isn't unique to satellite. Telecommunications operators, energy utilities, and industrial manufacturers have been navigating this convergence for years. The solution is always the same: unified operational intelligence that connects every sensor, every system, and every decision point into a single command environment. Quatro brings that proven operational intelligence pattern to satellite, with the depth of ground segment expertise and the breadth of vendor-agnostic connectivity that satellite operations requires.

*Framework note: This is the PAS "Problem" beat — names the pain the buyer already feels using industry-specific language (fragmented M&C/OSS/BSS, transponder anomalies, constellation scale). The specificity of the narrative establishes domain credibility and positions the convergence moment as a market reality, not a sales pitch.*

## Section 3 — PROBLEM SECTION (PAS — Agitation)

> **Framework rule:** Three-act temporal story with escalating consequence. This section should be PROSE, not cards or columns. Stories create urgency. Cards create comprehension. At the top of a landing page, urgency wins.

**Content (PAS — Agitation, escalating consequence):**

A ground operator spots an anomaly in the RF monitoring system — let's say a 2-degree frequency drift on transponder 7B. The tool alerts. The operator investigates the hardware logs. Thirty minutes later, the real cause is found: a software version mismatch in a peripheral modem. It's corrected. The transponder stabilizes.

But what happened in that 30 minutes? The customer connected to 7B experienced signal degradation. Their SLA clock started running. The backup capacity on Transponder 9A would have been cheaper and faster to activate, but the operator didn't know which customers were actually affected — that intelligence lived in a separate billing system, last updated four hours ago. The revenue impact isn't quantified until the next morning when accounting runs the SLA penalty calculation. By then, it's too late to file a service credit, so the revenue leakage gets recorded as a loss.

This is not one incident. This is the operational reality across satellite enterprises worldwide. Fragmented systems mean fragmented visibility. Fragmented visibility means reactive response. Reactive response means revenue loss, SLA breaches, and operational inefficiency that compounds across every ground station, every constellation, and every mission.

Quatro eliminates the gap. One utility connects every ground segment sensor, every fleet monitoring system, every billing and revenue system, and every space domain awareness feed into a single unified operational picture — with real-time anomaly detection, predictive impact assessment, and automated response orchestration that spans from the RF layer to the financial layer.

*Framework note: Agitation quantifies the asymmetry — a 30-minute outage cascades across disconnected systems, creating revenue loss that isn't even visible until hours later. Solution is positioned as the resolution (PAS "Solution" beat). Prose format per framework instruction.*
