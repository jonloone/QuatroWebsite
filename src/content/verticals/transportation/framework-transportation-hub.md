## Section 2 — INDUSTRY CONTEXT (2-3 sentences, "why now")

> **Framework rule:** Name the industry moment. Establish credibility: "this page was built by people who know our world."

**Label:** `THE LOGISTICS FRAGMENTATION CRISIS`

**Content (PAS — Problem, stated as industry moment):**

A container ship arrives at Posorja deep-water port at dawn. The port authority knows the berth is occupied. The logistics coordinator knows the trucker is delayed. The customs inspector knows the cargo manifests arrive by email. The regional coordinator knows a corridor incident happened 200 miles inland. But no one sees the full picture — because no system connects the port to the corridor to the destination.

This isn't unique to Ecuador or LATAM. Across every transportation network — maritime, aviation, highway — the same pattern repeats. Each node (port, airport, warehouse, toll station) holds a fragment of the logistics picture. Shipments that cross jurisdictional boundaries lose visibility. Response is reactive, fragmented, and slow. Delays cascade. Costs multiply. National resources aren't optimized.

*Framework note: This is the PAS "Problem" beat — names the pain using a concrete, emotionally resonant anecdote (Posorja container arrival). The specificity of the story and regional reference establishes transportation domain credibility.*
