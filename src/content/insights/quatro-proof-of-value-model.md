Enterprise software has a trust problem. Vendors promise transformation. Buyers get slide decks. Twelve months later, the pilot is still in "Phase 2" and nobody can point to a result.

We built the proof of value model to break that cycle. The premise is simple: pick one operational challenge, connect one system, and see intelligence working on your infrastructure in four weeks. The investment is credited toward your annual contract if you proceed.

## How it works

### Week 1-2: Connect and ingest

We connect to the system you identify — your SCADA historian, your network management platform, your core banking system, your fleet management tool. Whatever costs you the most to operate manually.

Quatro supports 8,000+ pre-built integrations. In most cases, connection is a configuration exercise, not an integration project. We tap into existing APIs, database connections, or protocol interfaces. Your source systems don't change. There's no downtime, no migration, no middleware to install.

By the end of week two, data is flowing from your operational systems into Quatro, normalized into a common model.

### Week 3: Intelligence and correlation

This is where the intelligence utility earns its keep. Quatro builds relationship models between your data streams, establishes operational baselines, and begins detecting patterns that your existing tools miss.

If you're an energy operator, that might mean correlating weather data with transformer load and maintenance history to predict equipment stress. If you're a telecom operator, it might mean linking fault events across terrestrial and satellite networks to reduce mean time to resolution. If you're a bank, it might mean reconciling customer records across 15 source systems into a unified identity.

The intelligence layer doesn't just aggregate data — it understands the relationships between operational events and surfaces the ones that matter.

### Week 4: Operator validation

Your operators work alongside the intelligence utility in shadow mode. They validate that the alerts are real, the correlations are meaningful, and the recommendations match their operational reality.

This is the most important week. We're not asking you to trust a demo — we're asking your operators to confirm that the intelligence matches what they know about your operation. Their feedback tunes the system and establishes the baseline for ongoing deployment.

## What you get at the end

After four weeks, you have:

A working deployment on your infrastructure — not a sandbox, not a demo environment. Your data, your systems, your operational context.

Validated intelligence that your operators have confirmed. Not theoretical value — demonstrated value on real operational data.

A clear picture of expansion. Once one system is connected, the compounding effect becomes obvious. Each additional data source deepens the intelligence picture. Patterns that were invisible in isolation become clear in correlation.

A commercial path forward. The proof of value investment is credited toward your annual contract. If the value isn't there, you walk away with the intelligence you've already gained.

## Why four weeks

Four weeks is long enough to connect real systems, build real intelligence, and validate real results. It's short enough that it fits within a single budget cycle, doesn't require executive committee approval at most organizations, and delivers a decision point before organizational momentum stalls.

The companies that move fastest typically identify their proof of value challenge in the first conversation. It's usually the problem that keeps coming up in operational reviews — the one where everyone knows the data exists somewhere but nobody can get to it fast enough to act.
