# xOps → Quatro Messaging Translation Map

**Internal Strategy Document — April 2026**
**Purpose:** Reference for translating Skyline/DataMiner xOps capabilities into Quatro's brand voice across all site pages, sales materials, and content.

---

## Core Positioning Shift

| Skyline Says | Quatro Says | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "The operating system for complex operational ecosystems" | "An intelligence network already built for your industry" | Skyline positions as infrastructure (OS). Quatro positions as outcomes (utility service). The buyer doesn't need to understand the architecture — they need to know it works for their industry. |
| "DataMiner xOps platform" | "Operational intelligence network" / "the network" | Never name DataMiner or xOps. Frame as an existing network the buyer connects to, not a platform they configure. |
| "xOps modules" | "Pre-built operational workflows" | The modules (work orders, maintenance, assets, SLA, scheduling) are the differentiator. They're why deployment is weeks, not months. Surface them as outcomes, not product features. |
| "Unified Digital Twin" | "One real-time picture of your entire operation" / "single operational truth" | Digital twin is technical jargon. The buyer outcome is: your team works from one screen instead of fifteen. |
| "Connectors catalog — 9,000+" | "9,000+ native integrations across 1,000+ vendors" | Lead with the number and the implication (your systems are already compatible). Never explain the connector architecture. |
| "Augmented Operations" | "Operator guidance" / "contextual intelligence" / "intelligence that meets your team where they work" | Skyline's term is product branding. Quatro's version puts the human first — the intelligence serves the operator, not the other way around. |
| "DataMiner Assistant (LLM)" | "Built-in intelligence" / "AI-native decision support" | Never name the DataMiner Assistant. Frame AI as a capability of the network, not a named product. |
| "Agent to Agent / MCP" | Do not reference | Internal architecture. Never surface on the Quatro site. |
| "MediaOps / GridOps / SatOps / NetOps / IoTOps" | "Energy" / "Networks" / "National Security" / "Critical Infrastructure" / "Finance" | White-label rules. Never use Skyline's vertical branding. Use Quatro's five industry pillars. |

## Capability Translation by Layer

*What Skyline calls it:* Connectors, protocol support, data collection, cloud data collection

|---|---|---|
| PLC, OPC UA, DNP3, Modbus, MQTT, BACnet, SNMP, REST, IEC x, PROFINET, ICCP | "Every major industrial protocol — SCADA, IoT, enterprise, cloud" | Homepage, platform page. Never list all protocols on the homepage — use the aggregate claim. Protocol lists belong on the platform/technical page. |
| Licensed spectrum (LTE-M, NB-IoT, 4G/5G-IoT) | "Cellular IoT connectivity" | Networks and Critical Infrastructure industry pages |
| Unlicensed spectrum (LoRaWAN, Sigfox, Zigbee, 6LoWPAN) | "Low-power sensor networks" | Energy, Critical Infrastructure pages |
| Satellite IoT | "Satellite-native connectivity" | Networks page, defense use cases |
| Cloud data collection (AWS, Azure) | "Cloud-native ingestion" | Finance page, platform page |
| "No agent required on controlled product" | "Agentless integration — no software installed on your equipment" | Technical buyers, platform page |
| 9,000+ connectors | "9,000+ native integrations across 1,000+ vendors" | Everywhere. This is the lead proof point. |

### Layer 3: xOps Modules (Business Process Layer)
*What Skyline calls it:* xOps modules — the pre-built business processes

**This is Quatro's biggest untapped messaging advantage.** These modules are why deployment takes weeks. Every competitor (Palantir, Splunk, ServiceNow) requires months of custom configuration to build these workflows. They already exist in the Quatro network.

| xOps Module | Quatro Messaging | Industry Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Infrastructure IDE | "Infrastructure modeling and configuration" | All — technical buyers |
| Plan & Build | "Capacity planning and infrastructure buildout" | Networks, Energy |
| Work Orders & Ticketing | "Automated work order routing and field coordination" | Energy (truck rolls), Critical Infrastructure (municipal ops) |
| Planned Maintenance | "Predictive maintenance scheduling" | Energy (turbines, pipelines), Networks (ground segments) |
| Assets Management | "Full asset lifecycle tracking" | All industries |
| Facility Management | "Facility operations intelligence" | Critical Infrastructure, Finance (branch ops) |
| People & Organization | "Team and shift management" | All — especially 24/7 operations |

| Contract Management | "Contract lifecycle and obligation tracking" | Finance, Networks (carrier agreements) |
| Cost & Billing | "Operational cost analysis and billing automation" | Networks (usage-based), Finance |

### Layer 4: DataMiner Intelligence (AI Layer)
*What Skyline calls it:* DataMiner Assistant, Agent to Agent, MCP

| xOps Capability | Quatro Messaging | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Root Cause Analysis | "Automated root cause analysis — your team stops investigating and starts intervening" | Strong for Energy (fault isolation) and Networks (service degradation) |
| Operational Insights | "Pattern recognition across your operation — anomalies surfaced before they become incidents" | All industries |
| Data Integrity | "Data quality monitoring — trusted data as the foundation" | Finance (regulatory), Critical Infrastructure (compliance) |
| Autonomous Operation | "Human-approved automation — the system recommends, your team decides" | Key differentiator. Per manifesto: the human is the hero. Never position as replacing operators. |

## The DataOS Amplifier Story

DataOS is NOT a separate product to sell. It's the natural expansion when the operational network reaches its ceiling.

### When to introduce DataOS in messaging:

| Buyer Signal | DataOS Message |
|---|---|
| "We need to connect operational data with our financial systems" | "DataOS adds enterprise intelligence — governed knowledge graphs that connect OT data with financial, commercial, and supply chain context." |
| "How do we get AI that understands our whole business, not just our sensors?" | "DataOS provides the semantic layer — ontologies that define what entities mean across your entire organization. Agents reason across operational AND business context." |
| "We have compliance requirements around data governance" | "DataOS adds PII discovery, data masking, quality SLOs, full lineage, and compliance reporting — the governance layer enterprise and defense customers require." |
| "We want to build data products our business teams can use" | "DataOS turns operational intelligence into governed data products — reusable, documented, and accessible to teams outside the control room." |

### The two-sentence pitch for DataOS expansion:

"The operational intelligence network handles what's happening right now — alarms, assets, workflows, the real-time picture. DataOS extends that into what it *means* for your business — connecting operational data with financial, commercial, and strategic context through governed knowledge graphs."

### What DataOS adds (never lead with this — always follow the operational network):

| Dimension | Operational Network Alone | + DataOS |
|---|---|---|
| Context | Operational — sensors, alarms, devices, services | + Enterprise — customer records, contracts, financials, supply chain |
| Reasoning | Pattern-matched against operational telemetry | + Grounded in semantic models with ontologies |
| Governance | Role-based access, audit trails | + PII discovery, data masking, quality SLOs, full lineage |
| Cross-domain | Correlates across monitored systems | + Correlates operational AND enterprise systems |
