# Quatro Image Generation — Style Brief v7

**Style updated:** April 2026
**Key shift from v6:** From isolated macro object portraits → **cinematic editorial photography of industry worlds**. Pull back. Show the environment, the infrastructure ecosystem, the broader landscape. Each image should feel like a frame from a documentary — you're inside the world of that industry, surrounded by its reality. Warm, human, grounded.

**Reference:** IBM Design Language photography (documentary, editorial, authentic) — but warmer. IBM avoids golden hour; we embrace it. IBM is cool and clinical; we're warm analog film. Think IBM's composition discipline + Samsara's warmth and reverence.

---

## Creative Philosophy

Every Quatro image is a **cinematic frame from inside an industry's world**. Not a zoomed-in texture study. Not a stock photo of a facility. A moment captured by a documentary filmmaker who is embedded in that industry — standing on the factory floor, riding the rail line, walking through the ground station at dawn.

The viewer should feel **immersed in a real environment** that represents the full lifecycle of that industry. They should feel the scale, the systems, the orchestration. The image tells a story: this is what this industry looks like when it's running.

**The sweet spot:** You're close enough to feel the environment's character (the light, the surfaces, the atmosphere) but pulled back enough to understand the SYSTEM. You see infrastructure in context — not a single bolt, but the machine it belongs to. Not a single fiber, but the cable tray full of hundreds.

**Reference points:**
- **IBM Design Language** — editorial authenticity, 2x grid composition, step-back perspective that shows context and environment. Their principle of being an "objective observer of the working world"
- **Ron Fricke (Samsara/Koyaanisqatsi)** — systems as poetry. Infrastructure as meditation. The mundane made transcendent through beautiful cinematography
- **Christopher Payne** — manufacturing process as visual drama. The beauty of environments where things are made
- **Edward Burtynsky** — industrial landscapes that reveal pattern, scale, and the sublime in infrastructure

But WARMER than all of them. Quatro's palette leans into golden hour, amber practical light, and analog film warmth.

## The Film Look

Cinematic documentary, graded like Kodak Portra 800:

- **35mm or 50mm lens** — environmental, immersive, natural perspective (NOT 85mm macro anymore)
- **f/2.8–f/4 aperture** — enough depth of field to show environment context, but still with pleasing bokeh on distant elements
- **Kodak Portra 800 character** — warm undertones, fine organic grain, rich shadow detail, slightly muted highlights
- **Green-shifted shadows** — all shadow areas push toward forest green (#0f4c3a)
- **Warm amber highlights** — practical light sources carry golden warmth (#F4A261)
- **Visible film grain** — organic, fine, like a high-res 35mm scan
- **Natural vignetting** — subtle, drawing eye to the compositional anchor

**What this is NOT:**
- Not extreme macro / object portraits (that was v6)
- Not compressed, dithered, or halftoned (the site's Bayer shader handles that)
- Not stock photography — no generic, no staged, no clipart quality
- Not illustration, CGI, or digital art
- Not oversaturated or HDR
- Not overly abstract — the viewer should understand what industry they're looking at

## Composition: The IBM Grid, But Warmer

Borrow IBM's compositional discipline:

- **Divisions of two** — split the frame horizontally or vertically into two zones. Subject in one, environment in the other
- **Clear focal anchor** — every image has one primary element that draws the eye. Infrastructure element, light source, or convergence point
- **Step-back framing** — show enough environment that the viewer understands the WORLD, not just the object. You're standing inside the space
- **Eye-level or slightly elevated** — natural, documentary perspective. Occasionally overhead for pattern/scale
- **Sharp focus throughout** — f/2.8–f/4 keeps both foreground interest and background context readable (unlike the f/1.8 macro blur of v6)

**Composition don'ts (per IBM):**
- No empty centers
- No conflicting focal points
- No perspectives that are neither eye-level nor aerial (no weird Dutch angles)
- No artificially manufactured scenes

## The Industry Landscape Approach

Each industry hero should feel like you're INSIDE that industry's world. The image captures the broader ecosystem — not one piece of hardware, but the environment where that hardware lives and operates.

**The test:** Can someone in that industry look at the hero and say "yes, that's my world"? Not "that's a picture of a bolt from my world." The whole world.

### Energy
- **The world:** Containerized battery storage facilities in industrial yards. Smart grid control centers with wall-to-wall displays. Offshore wind farms from service vessel perspective. EV supercharger hubs at dusk. Hydrogen electrolysis interiors. HVDC converter stations. The infrastructure of energy in 2026 is distributed, dense, and sensored — not pastoral power lines at sunset
- **Mood:** Technical, vast, quietly powerful. The physical layer of renewable integration and grid intelligence
- **Light:** Hard midday sun on outdoor facilities. Screen glow in control centers. Dawn mist on water-adjacent infrastructure. Safety amber from warning systems and practical lighting

### Networks & Telecommunications
- **The world:** Rooftop 5G small cell arrays at golden hour. Submarine cable landing stations. Network operations centers at night. Fiber splicing trucks on rural roads. Internet exchange points with dense cross-connect panels. Edge compute enclosures. The invisible distributed infrastructure that makes connectivity real — not a single heroic tower but dozens of interconnected nodes
- **Mood:** Cool precision with warm practical accents. The hidden infrastructure of connectivity made visible
- **Light:** Golden hour on outdoor equipment. Teal LED status light mixed with warm overhead in NOCs. Twilight sky behind antenna silhouettes

### Smart Cities
- **The world:** IoT sensor poles carrying multiple instrumentation packages (air quality, traffic radar, 5G small cells, environmental sensors) on urban intersections in clear daylight. Smart traffic operations centers. Digital twin visualization rooms. Autonomous vehicle testing corridors. Smart waste management systems. The sensor layer woven into everyday infrastructure — visible when you know where to look
- **Mood:** Urban intelligence. The city's nervous system made visible through instrumentation density
- **Light:** Clear afternoon daylight showing sensor hardware detail. Mixed urban light for night operations centers. Cyber emerald status indicators on active sensor packages

### Defense & Intelligence
- **The world:** ISR drone on a tarmac at dawn. Satellite ground station arrays in remote desert. Secure operations centers with displays casting blue light. Radar installations silhouetted against sky. Perimeter security infrastructure. Military comm antenna farms
- **Mood:** Cold, sovereign, deliberate. Controlled environments and vast awareness. Zero ornament
- **Light:** Pre-dawn blue. Cold fluorescent from operations centers. No warmth — this is the one industry where we suppress amber

### Industrial & Manufacturing
- **The world:** Collaborative manufacturing cells where humans and cobots work side by side without safety cages. Digital twin dashboard rooms showing real-time factory state. Metal 3D printing bays. AGV fleets navigating warehouse corridors. Predictive maintenance sensor arrays on critical equipment. The factory of 2026 is integrated, instrumented, and increasingly autonomous
- **Mood:** Mechanical sublime meets digital precision. Human-machine partnership, not replacement
- **Light:** High-intensity LED arrays (500+ lux) mixed with natural light from high factory windows. Safety amber on equipment markings. Cyber emerald status indicators on robot controllers

### Finance & Governance
- **The world:** Financial cybersecurity operations centers glowing with display walls. Co-location facility floors with FPGA clusters and fiber cross-connects. Payment processing nodes with redundant server grids. Quantitative research labs with triple-monitor stations and glass-walled compute closets. The infrastructure of modern finance is silicon, fiber, and real-time data — not marble and vaults
- **Mood:** Silent, algorithmic, high-stakes. The nerve center of institutional trust expressed through monitoring density and technical precision
- **Light:** Screen glow dominant — cool teal from data visualizations mixing with warm brass task lighting. Polished concrete reflects display walls. Night operations aesthetic

### Transportation & Logistics
- **The world:** Automated container ports with 60-meter gantry cranes and autonomous ground vehicles. Drone delivery hubs. Fleet management operations centers. Smart highway corridors with embedded sensors. Cold chain monitoring facilities. Rail traffic management centers overlooking automated yards. The orchestration of global logistics by sensor networks and autonomous systems
- **Mood:** Scale and precision. The automated choreography of global trade operating itself
- **Light:** Mixed sodium vapor amber and cool LED arrays at dusk. Signal light reflections on steel and container surfaces. Screen glow in operations centers

### Water & Environment
- **The world:** Water treatment facilities with settling tanks in morning light. River monitoring stations. Reservoir spillways with water flowing. Irrigation control structures. Watershed measurement infrastructure in natural settings
- **Mood:** Living systems. Natural and engineered flow working together. Measured, cyclical
- **Light:** Natural backlit — morning light through water, dappled forest light on monitoring equipment. Emerald and green dominant

### Broadcast & Media
- **The world:** Virtual production stages with massive curved LED walls (270°, 50m+ wide) where physical sets merge with virtual environments. IP-based SMPTE 2110 master control rooms. Cloud playout centers. SNG satellite uplink trucks deployed at live events. 8K camera systems on production sets. AI-driven automated production workflows. The broadcast infrastructure of 2026 is IP-native, cloud-distributed, and LED-powered
- **Mood:** The seam between physical and virtual. Technical craft in service of content creation at scale
- **Light:** LED wall illumination mixing with tungsten production lighting. Screen glow in master control. Amber from practical stage lighting. The light itself is content

## Image Tiers

| Tier | Dimensions | Composition |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Hero** | 1920×1080 | Wide environmental — the industry's world. Clear focal anchor, step-back framing, space for text overlay on one side |
| **Capability** | 800×600 | Closer environmental detail — a specific subsystem or process within the broader world. Still shows context |
| **Challenge** | 400×400 | The same environment but showing degradation, failure, or friction. Dark, desaturated, things not working |
| **Solution Hero** | 1920×1080 | Same as Hero tier |

## Challenge Images: The System Under Stress

Same industry environments, but captured in a state of degradation or failure:

- **Aging infrastructure:** Rusted equipment, cracked surfaces, outdated panels with analog gauges
- **Darkness:** Environments where lights are off, screens dark, systems powered down
- **Isolation:** Empty control rooms, unmanned stations, abandoned equipment
- **Disorder:** Cable tangles, misaligned equipment, cluttered work areas
- **Environmental stress:** Fog/haze reducing visibility, water intrusion, dust accumulation

**Grading shift:** More desaturated, higher contrast, colder. Forest green shadows go deeper toward near-black. Amber warmth is absent or dying.

## Hard Rules

1. **Show the world, not the widget.** Pull back enough to see the industry environment in context
2. **Every image tells an industry story.** Someone in that industry should recognize their world
3. **Sharp throughout.** f/2.8–f/4, not f/1.8 macro blur. Context matters — keep background readable
4. **Warm analog film.** Portra 800 character. Green shadows, amber practical light
5. **Natural/practical lighting only.** Golden hour, facility lights, instrument panels, signal lamps — no studio setups
6. **Documentary authenticity.** Like IBM's "objective observer" — no manufactured scenes, no obvious AI generation
7. **No typography/logos/words.** Ever
8. **No identifiable human faces.** People can appear as silhouettes, motion blur, or from behind — showing scale
9. **Challenge images = same world, degraded state.** Dark, desaturated, systems failing
10. **The dither shader handles the texture.** Photos should be sharp and gorgeous

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Extreme macro of single objects (that was v6 — too isolated, too abstract)
- Generic environments (a "data center" that could be any brand's photo)
- Stock photography staging — no posed scenes, no obvious setup
- Zoomed-out aerial establishing shots (too impersonal)
- HDR, oversaturated, or digitally-rendered look
- Neural network / brain / AI imagery
- Text, labels, or HUD overlays baked into the image
- Pure abstraction where you can't identify the industry
