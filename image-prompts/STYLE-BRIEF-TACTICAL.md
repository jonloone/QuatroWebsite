# Quatro Image Generation — Tactical Intelligence Style (v7t)

**Style created:** April 2026
**Relationship to v7:** This is a **complementary lane** — not a replacement. The v7 documentary/Portra 800 style remains the primary art direction for hero images, editorial photography, and immersive industry scenes. The Tactical Intelligence style is for images that need to show **the intelligence layer itself** — data in motion, networked systems, operational HUDs, and the digital substrate that connects physical infrastructure.

**When to use v7 (documentary):** Hero backgrounds, editorial sections, proof points, any image where the viewer should feel *inside* the physical world of an industry.

**When to use v7t (tactical):** Capability visuals, solution diagrams, network topology illustrations, any image where the viewer should feel *inside the operational intelligence layer* — seeing what operators see, understanding how systems connect.

---

## Creative Philosophy

If v7 is the documentary filmmaker embedded on the factory floor, v7t is the **operations center at 2 AM** — the view from inside the command layer. These images show the world as data: connected, instrumented, alive with signal. But they never feel cold or sterile. The warmth comes from amber light sources, from the suggestion that human operators are just off-frame, from the physical infrastructure glowing through the digital overlay.

**The sweet spot:** Real infrastructure rendered through a tactical lens. Not abstract data visualization. Not sci-fi concept art. The viewer should recognize actual operational subjects (vehicles, antennas, pipelines, city grids) while simultaneously seeing the intelligence layer that connects them.

**Reference points:**
- **Military HUD interfaces** — zone labels, status panels, connection arcs — but civilian and warm
- **Isometric tactical displays** — the god's-eye operational view, network topology made visual
- **Halftone/dithered treatments** — analog warmth applied to digital subjects, bridging physical and data layers
- **Bloomberg terminal aesthetics** — dense information, dark backgrounds, purposeful color coding

## The Visual Language

### Color System: The Teal-Amber Axis

The entire palette lives on a single axis from cool intelligence to warm infrastructure:

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| **Substrate** | Near-black / dark slate | `#0a0f14` to `#1a1f2e` | Backgrounds, base layer |
| **Data lines** | Cyan / teal | `#00d4aa` to `#0ff0c0` | Connection arcs, network paths, data flow |
| **HUD panels** | Deep blue | `#1a3a6a` to `#2255aa` | Zone labels, status readouts, info panels |
| **Infrastructure** | Warm amber / orange | `#e8852e` to `#f4a261` | Physical assets (vehicles, buildings, equipment) |
| **Alert / active** | Bright amber | `#ffb627` | Active states, key metrics, emphasis |
| **Shadow / depth** | Forest green-black | `#0a1f18` | Depth layering, environmental shadow |

**The rule:** Cyan/teal = data and intelligence. Amber/orange = physical world and infrastructure. The two meeting is the whole point — that's the integration story.

### Texture & Treatment

- **Halftone / dithered grain** — not smooth CGI gradients. The texture should feel like a risograph print or vintage radar display. This adds analog warmth to digital subjects
- **Subtle scan lines or noise** — particularly on the HUD panels and data overlays, suggesting a live display
- **Glow and bloom** — data lines and active elements emit soft light into the surrounding darkness, creating depth
- **Dark backgrounds** — the substrate is always dark (near-black, deep navy, or dark slate), allowing the data and infrastructure to read clearly
- **Depth through light** — no flat illustrations. Light sources (amber from infrastructure, cyan from data) create dimensional depth

### Composition

- **Elevated or isometric perspective** — the tactical/operational view. Looking down at infrastructure networks, fleet patterns, city grids. Not eye-level immersion (that's v7's territory)
- **Network topology visible** — connection lines, arcs, or paths between assets should be explicit, not implied
- **HUD overlay panels** — 2-4 floating info panels with zone labels, status indicators, or data readouts. These should have: thin border lines (1-2px), slightly translucent backgrounds, small monospace text that suggests data without being readable
- **Asymmetric density** — one area of the frame should be information-dense (the operational core), while another area breathes (where a headline or label could sit)
- **No center-weighted compositions** — anchor the operational density to one side or quadrant

---

## What This Style Is NOT

- **Not sci-fi concept art** — no spaceships, no Tron grids, no fantasy technology. Every subject should be recognizable real-world infrastructure
- **Not flat illustration** — must have dimensional depth from lighting, not just color
- **Not clean/minimal UI mockup** — the HUD panels should feel lived-in, operational, slightly dense — not a Figma wireframe
- **Not cold** — the amber warmth is non-negotiable. If an image feels like a sterile tech demo, it needs more warmth
- **Not literal screenshot** — these are stylized representations of operational views, not actual software UI captures

## Per-Vertical Translations

### SATCOM & Space
- **Physical layer (amber):** Ground station dishes, antenna arrays, satellite silhouettes, orbital paths
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Signal paths as luminous arcs from dish to sky, frequency allocation panels, link budget readouts, coverage zone boundaries
- **Composition:** Ground-up perspective showing dish with signal arcs rising to orbital assets, or overhead showing ground station network with interconnecting signal paths
- **Texture note:** The halftone treatment works especially well here — it evokes vintage radar displays and analog signal processing

### Defense & Security
- **Physical layer (amber):** Perimeter fencing, vehicle patrols, base infrastructure, sensor masts
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Perimeter zones with classification labels, patrol route arcs, sensor coverage overlaps, threat detection radii
- **Composition:** Overhead tactical view of a secured perimeter with layered detection zones. HUD panels showing sector status
- **Texture note:** Lean heavier into the military HUD aesthetic — more angular panel borders, more abbreviated label text

### Energy & Utilities
- **Physical layer (amber):** Pipeline corridors, substations, turbine arrays, SCADA cabinets
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Flow direction indicators along pipelines, grid load balance arcs between substations, sensor health status panels at junction points
- **Composition:** Linear infrastructure (pipeline, transmission line) running through the frame with data overlays at regular intervals showing monitoring state
- **Texture note:** Slightly warmer overall — energy infrastructure at dusk with amber practical lights on equipment

### Smart Cities & Municipal
- **Physical layer (amber):** Fleet vehicles (waste collection, transit, emergency), water infrastructure, traffic systems
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Route optimization paths connecting fleet assets, zone assignments with boundary lines, real-time status panels per zone
- **Composition:** The fleet network image is the archetype — overhead view of city/zone grid with connected assets and zone intelligence panels
- **Texture note:** More color variation allowed — urban environments can introduce building lights, street glow

### Transportation & Logistics
- **Physical layer (amber):** Cargo vessels, port cranes, rail cars, container yards
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Vessel tracking arcs, berth allocation zones, supply chain flow paths, customs/clearance status panels
- **Composition:** Port or rail yard from elevated perspective with flow paths showing cargo movement and status overlays at transfer points
- **Texture note:** Dense, busy — logistics environments should feel information-rich

### Networks & Telecommunications
- **Physical layer (amber):** Cell towers, fiber junction boxes, data centers, cable landing stations
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Network topology paths between nodes, bandwidth utilization halos around towers, latency indicators on link paths
- **Composition:** Network map view — nodes as physical assets connected by luminous data paths with health/status panels
- **Texture note:** The cleanest of the verticals — network infrastructure is itself orderly, so the HUD overlay can be more structured

### Finance & Banking
- **Physical layer (amber):** Branch building silhouettes, ATM networks, data center racks
- **Intelligence layer (teal):** Transaction flow arcs between nodes, risk score panels, compliance status zones, customer cluster boundaries
- **Composition:** Network topology of financial system nodes with transaction flows and risk indicators
- **Texture note:** More blue-shifted than other verticals — financial imagery leans cooler, but still maintains the amber accent for physical infrastructure

## Prompt Template for Tactical Intelligence Images

When writing generation prompts for this style, use the following structure:

```
[PERSPECTIVE]: Elevated isometric / overhead tactical / 30-degree downward angle

[PHYSICAL LAYER]: {Describe the real-world infrastructure assets in warm amber/orange tones — what equipment, vehicles, or structures are visible}

[INTELLIGENCE LAYER]: {Describe the data overlay — glowing cyan/teal connection arcs, zone boundaries, flow paths between assets}

[HUD PANELS]: {Describe 2-4 floating information panels with thin borders, translucent dark backgrounds, zone labels or status readouts in small text}

[SUBSTRATE]: Dark background — near-black slate with subtle grid texture or topographic suggestion

[LIGHTING]: {Amber glow from physical infrastructure elements, cyan emission from data lines and HUD panels, soft bloom where light meets darkness}

[TEXTURE]: Halftone/dithered grain treatment. Subtle scan line noise on HUD panels. Not smooth CGI — analog warmth over digital subject matter.

[COLOR AXIS]: Teal (#00d4aa) for intelligence/data. Amber (#e8852e to #f4a261) for physical infrastructure. Deep blue (#1a3a6a) for HUD panels. Near-black (#0a0f14) substrate.

[COMPOSITION NOTE]: {Where the density lives, where the breathing room is for text overlay if needed}
```

## Mixing v7 and v7t on the Same Page

A page can use both styles effectively:

- **Hero section:** v7 documentary (immersive, warm, human)
- **Capability/solution section:** v7t tactical (showing the intelligence layer, the connected system)
- **Proof point:** v7 documentary (back to the real world, the outcome)
- **Dark "how it works" section:** v7t tactical (the operational view)

This creates a natural rhythm: *you're in the world → you see the intelligence layer → you're back in the world, transformed.*

---

## Image Types Best Suited for v7t

1. **Network topology visuals** — showing how assets connect across geography
2. **Capability illustrations** — what the intelligence layer reveals that's invisible to the naked eye
3. **Solution architecture visuals** — how data flows through an integrated system
4. **Dark section backgrounds** — moody, information-rich backgrounds for "how it works" sections
5. **Homepage "Connect Operate Command" section** — showing the three modes as tactical views
6. **Before/after contrasts** — "without intelligence" (dark, disconnected) vs. "with intelligence" (lit up, connected)
