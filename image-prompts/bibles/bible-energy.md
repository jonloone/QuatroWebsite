# Energy — Visual World Bible

## The World in 2026

Modern energy infrastructure is a distributed, dense network of sensing, storage, and conversion. The physical landscape shows containerized battery systems stacked in industrial parks, wind farms rotating at grid scale, EV charging stations spreading across streetscapes, and control centers where dispatch operators watch real-time flow balancing. Energy in 2026 is visible—cables, boxes, dishes, ceramics—because the stakes are too high to hide it.

## Hero Scenes (1920×1080)

**Smart Grid Control Center at Dawn**
Interior of a utility operations center: floor-to-wall video walls displaying grid topology, load flow, and renewable injection rates. Operators at ergonomic stations with multiple monitors. The space is cool-lit (blues from screens) mixing with amber task lighting on desks. Large format geographic map showing substations and transmission lines. Cable management overhead. Early morning light leaking through small windows, but the room is lit primarily by screen glow.

**Battery Energy Storage Facility Exterior**
Outdoor industrial yard containing 8-12 stacked shipping container modules (Tesla Megapack or equivalent form factor) arranged in rows. White/grey containers with visible cooling systems and electrical connections. Surrounding chain-link fence with high-voltage warning signage. Clear day with hard shadows from the containers. Nearby transformer pad and overhead transmission lines visible. Foreground shows access road and maintenance equipment.

**Offshore Wind Farm from Supply Vessel**
View from a service vessel approaching an offshore wind installation. Massive turbine towers (monopile or jacket foundation visible above water), rotors spinning at different phases. Rough sea state (2-3 meter swells), grey overcast sky, white foam. Turbine nacelles and hub details visible through spray and distance. Cables and subsea infrastructure suggested by marker buoys.

**EV Supercharger Hub at Dusk**
Tesla V4 or equivalent supercharger station: 8-16 columns arranged in clean rows, each with two attached cables. Modern canopy structure overhead. Parking spaces behind. Surrounding light poles with integrated 5G small cells (pizza-box shaped). Vehicle(s) actively charging. Golden hour light from the west creating long shadows, mixing with cool LED cabin lighting from nearby buildings. Digital signage showing pricing and availability.

**Hydrogen Electrolysis Facility Interior**
Industrial electrolyzer stack room: tall white/stainless steel modules arranged in production array. Water inlet/outlet piping in manifold configurations. Visible instrumentation (pressure gauges, digital readouts). Industrial flooring. Overhead lighting. Dehumidification systems visible. The space feels clean, pressurized, technical. Workers in PPE moving between modules (blurred to suggest scale/motion).

**HVDC Converter Station Equipment Room**
Indoor equipment housing: large cylindrical or rectangular capacitor banks, valve groups on pedestals, cooling system piping. Thermal imaging camera view showing heat distribution, OR natural light showing the hardware detail. Catwalk or access platform visible for scale. Ceramic insulators on standoff hardware. Cable terminations overhead. Industrial utility aesthetic—built for reliability, not appearance.

**Solar Tracking Field Detail**
Close view of 3-4 heliostats or single-axis tracking arrays mid-morning. Each tracker showing the drive system, mounting frame, and ceramic insulators on the support structure. Adjustable angle visible. Parallel rows receding into background. Shadows cast by frames on the ground. Blue sky above. Mount points and servo mechanisms visible.

**Substation Transformer Yard at Midday**
Outdoor transformer installation: large oil-filled transformer (2-3 meters tall), ceramic bushing insulators in porcelain, visible cooling radiators. Disconnect switches and lightning arresters overhead. Metal framework and cable runs. Gravel or concrete ground. Metal lattice transmission tower visible in background. Clear, harsh lighting showing all the industrial geometry.

**Smart Grid Sensor on Distribution Pole**
Close detail of an instrument transformer or PMU (Phasor Measurement Unit) mounted on a utility pole. The sensor/device is mounted near the top, showing attachment points, cable routing, and some visible electronics/displays. Surrounding utility attachments (other sensors, conduit). Pole visible below and above. Communication dish or antenna nearby for data transmission. Afternoon sunlight.

**Battery Thermal Management Loop**
Detail of BESS container interior: visible cooling piping manifolds, thermal sensors, electrical distribution board, battery modules in rows. The interconnection detail between modules. Fluorescent or industrial LED task lighting. Metallic and polymeric components clearly visible. The organized chaos of industrial systems designed for 20-year lifespan.

**Wind Turbine Nacelle Interior**
Interior machinery space (gearbox, generator assembly) as seen from climbing up the tower or via inspection access. Large cylindrical components, cable trays overhead, transformer units, control boxes mounted on walls. Industrial workspace lit by portable lights or small windows. Scale suggested by handrails and access panels.

**EV Charging Station Electrical Panel**
Close-up of the power distribution and safety equipment at a supercharger: main disconnect, contactors, protection devices, metering equipment, thermal management pump (visible), control electronics. Cable terminations. Industrial rack arrangement. Technical labels and warnings. Cool fluorescent or LED lighting showing component detail.

**Renewable Curtailment Display Monitor**
Control room monitor showing real-time dispatch dashboard: curtailment signals being sent to solar or wind sites, acceptance/rejection status, financial impact calculations, forecasted generation vs. actual, balancing services marketplace. Multiple windows and data visualizations. Operator hands or reflection visible. Blue-dominant color palette from screen.

## Challenge Scenes (400×400)

**Battery Storage Module Degradation**
Close detail of a BESS container showing visible aging: discoloration on exterior paint, thermal runaway damage evidence (scorch marks, warped panels), failing gasket or seal (white residue), offline status indication (red light/flag). Surrounding containers still operational but this one clearly compromised. Industrial yard setting.

**Substation Transformer Overload State**
Transformer showing stress indicators: visible oil seeping (dark staining on radiator), temperature gauge in red zone (extreme reading visible), cooling fans running at maximum (noise/vibration implied), nearby emergency response equipment staged. Clear indication of grid stress event.

**Wind Turbine Feathering for Excessive Wind**
Turbine in storm conditions (dark, low visibility, rain visible), blades in feather position (parallel to wind), clearly NOT generating despite capacity. Surrounding turbines also stationary. Grey-black sky. Metal fatigue or operational constraint clearly evident.

**Charging Station Power Deficit**
Supercharger station with multiple columns showing "no availability" or "queue" status, several vehicles waiting (frustrated drivers visible through windows), broken cable or offline indicator on one column, electrical system struggling to meet demand. Midday harsh sun showing the strain.

**Grid Instability Display**
Control room monitor showing frequency excursion red lines, cascading alarms, load shedding in progress, interconnect flows at critical limits, multiple regions highlighted in alert colors. Operator stress (posture, facial expression) visible. Tense lighting, shadows.

## Color & Light Profile

Energy infrastructure lives in **technical light**—a mix of industrial fluorescents, LED task lighting, and the harsh shadows of outdoor equipment in direct sun.

**Indoor (Control Centers, Equipment Rooms):**
- Primary: Cool blue-white LED (5000K+) from monitors and overhead systems
- Accent: Safety Amber (#F4A261) task lighting on work surfaces and warning systems
- Shadow: Near Black (#0A1128) in cable troughs and equipment base enclosures
- Metallic warmth: Brass (#C4944A) visible on transformer casings and copper bus work

**Outdoor (Fields, Yards, Substations):**
- Dominant: Clear sky blue or overcast grey (depending on weather)
- Highlight: Cyber Emerald (#00D1B2) in control cable jackets, status indicator lights
- Earth: Forest Green (#0f4c3a) in surrounding vegetation, shadow areas under equipment
- Metal: Brass (#C4944A) natural weathering on copper/brass hardware
- Stress: Safety Amber (#F4A261) in warning signage, emergency lighting, thermal imagery

**Thermal/Night:**
- Heat signature reds and oranges on cooling radiators
- Cyber Emerald (#00D1B2) status LEDs in darkness
- Safety Amber (#F4A261) perimeter lighting and emergency beacons

## Visual Don'ts

- **Power lines at sunset** — cliché pastoral imagery that obscures real infrastructure
- **Generic "energy" = power plant cooling towers** — outdated coal-era imagery, not 2026 distribution
- **Glowing grid maps without physical environment** — abstract without grounding
- **Dramatic storm lightning around turbines** — fear-mongering and unrealistic for operational situations

### Routing Rules

**Blog Post Matching:**
- Analyze the blog post's primary topic and keywords against the `topic_keywords` column
- Select the scene with the highest keyword overlap
- Prefer exact match over partial match (e.g., "transformer overload" matches Substation Transformer Overload State before Substation Transformer Yard at Midday)

**When Multiple Scenes Match:**
1. **For how-it-works / technical deep-dive content** → Use the **Capability tier** scene for detailed, instructional imagery showing specific equipment and instrumentation
2. **For problem-statement / failure mode content** → Use the **Challenge tier** scene to ground the pain point in visual reality
3. **For overview / thought-leadership / industry context content** → Use the **Hero tier** scene for expansive, system-level perspective

**Challenge Scene Photography Rules:**
- Inherit the camera direction and composition grid rules from the corresponding hero or capability scene (e.g., Transformer Overload follows Substation Transformer Yard's eye-level step-back and vertical split)
- Apply degraded lighting: reduce saturation by 30–40%, introduce shadow pools, shift color temperature toward cool grey
- Shift accent color to Safety Amber (#F4A261) for warning emphasis and desaturate supporting Cyber Emerald or Brass elements
- Maintain industrial technical aesthetic while signaling operational distress through visual cues (gauge readings in red, visible damage, emergency equipment staging)
